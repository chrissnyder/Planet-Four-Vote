August 17, 2014 is the Northern Autumnal Equinox on Mars which signals the beginning of Spring on the Red Planet's South Pole and the start of the next season of monitoring by the HiRISE camera aboard the Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter. The sun will heat the carbon dioxide ice cap, that formed over the South Pole during the winter, producing carbon dioxide jets. These jets carry dust and dirt from the ground below to the top of the ice sheet where we think depending on the surface winds create dark fans and blotches that are seen from orbit. Soon hundreds of thousands of these dark seasonal features will dot the ice sheet and be captured in HiRISE images. With Planet Four, we ask for your help in marking the sizes and positions of these features in order to better study the Martian climate.

Earlier this year Planet Four has focused on mapping three areas on Mar's South Pole dubbed 'Ithaca', 'Inca City', and 'Manhattan'. To celebrate the equinox and the start of a new season of the HiRISE monitoring campaign, we're doing something special. In collaboration with the HiRISE team, we are offering a sneak peak, where you can decide which of these three areas will have its first Season 5 observation publicly released by the HiRISE team after it is taken. Who knows what we will see, perhaps there will already be fans.

Vote (as often as you like) for your favorite region below today. Voting will be open from August 1, 2014 9am GMT - September 1, 2014 9am GMT. We will be announcing the winning region on September 2nd, and in the meantime help us study the climate of Mars by mapping seasonal fans and blotches at http://www.planetfour.org
